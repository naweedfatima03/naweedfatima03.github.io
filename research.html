Research

Selected research from my time at the Computer Vision & Graphics Lab (CVGL) and the Centre for Urban Informatics, Technology and Policy (CITY), focusing on early-warning vision systems and geospatial methods for urban green space mapping.

WWF Early-Warning Forest Fire Vision System

WWF Initiative · CVGL, LUMS · Supervised by Dr. Murtaza Taj

Developed a real-time remote forest monitoring system combining a distributed PTZ camera network with edge-based fire–smoke detection to provide early alerts in vulnerable regions.

Early-warning systems Edge inference Environmental monitoring

PTZ Camera Tracking & Actuation Model

WWF Initiative · CVGL, LUMS · Supervised by Dr. Murtaza Taj

Implemented a PTZ tracking model that maps image-plane detections to real-world pan–tilt actuation, enabling automatic zoom and re-targeting for higher-resolution fire–smoke verification.

Camera geometry PTZ control Tracking-by-detection

Haze Removal via Dark Channel Prior

WWF Initiative · CVGL, LUMS · Supervised by Dr. Murtaza Taj

Designed a Dark Channel Prior–based dehazing module to improve visibility and downstream detection performance in foggy and low-contrast forest monitoring imagery.

Image restoration Dehazing Pre-processing for CV

Geospatial Dataset for Lahore’s Urban Green Space

Urban Green Space Initiative · CITY, LUMS · Supervised by Dr. Zubair Khalid

Constructed a labelled geospatial dataset for Lahore’s green spaces by fusing Google Maps vegetation masks, OpenStreetMap land-use polygons, and ESA WorldCover layers into a reproducible mapping workflow.

Remote sensing Geospatial data QGIS & GEE

Geographically Generalisable Urban Green Space Segmentation

Urban Green Space Initiative · CITY, LUMS · Supervised by Dr. Zubair Khalid

Investigated geographically generalisable methods for urban green-space segmentation, fine-tuning YOLOv11n-seg on high-resolution imagery and evaluating transfer to contrasting landscapes across Pakistan.

Semantic segmentation Domain generalisation Satellite imagery

Cartesian: GAN-Based Attention for Green Space Detection

Urban Green Space Initiative · CITY, LUMS · Supervised by Dr. Zubair Khalid

Developed Cartesian, a GAN-based semantic segmentation framework with variance- and Gaussian-based attention modules to improve fine-grained urban green space detection in very high-resolution imagery.

GANs Attention mechanisms Environmental mapping
